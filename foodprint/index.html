Diet Builder

Select a diet from the dropdown menu or build your own. The diet will then be projected to be the average diet for a planet with a population of 10 billion humans, as per the Eat-Lancet study.
Toggle between average and worst-case environmental impact estimates.

Land Use

Total land required to produce your diet, scaled to 10 billion people. Select a country for scale.
Green silhouettes are within the EAT-Lancet target, red ones exceed it. Land use is measured in meters squared (m²) for one year to produce one kilogram of a given food product.
Data is taken from Poore and Nemecek, and processed by Our World in Data.

GHG Emissions

Greenhouse gas emissions are measured in millions of tonnes of carbon dioxide-equivalents emitted per day (this means non-CO₂ gases are weighted by theamount of warming they cause over a 100-year timescale), projected on 10 billion humans. The red line shows the emissions goal proposed by the EAT-Lancet Commission for 2050 in the Planetary Health Diet Report.
Data is taken from Poore and Nemecek, and processed by Our World in Data.

Water Use

Freshwater withdrawals used to produce your diet in km³ per day at global scale. The red plane marks the EAT-Lancet sustainable water use boundary for 2050.
Data is taken from Poore and Nemecek, and processed by Our World in Data.

Animal Deaths

Animals killed in real time to sustain your diet scaled to 10 billion people.
Data taken from Funalytics.
Please note that the number of fish and sealife deaths are difficult to estimate, but even so, they would average around a trillion per year for the average fish consumption, and thus be extremely difficult to display.
